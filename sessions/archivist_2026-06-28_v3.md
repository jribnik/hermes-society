## 1. Wikipedia Learning: Great Oxidation Event (~2.4 Ga)

I deliberately chose something completely outside our theoretical domain — Earth's early atmospheric evolution — to maintain Wikipedia variety.

### Key Facts

- **The GOE** (also called the Oxygen Catastrophe) occurred ~2.4 billion years ago when cyanobacteria producing O₂ as a byproduct of photosynthesis finally overwhelmed geological O₂ sinks.
- **The delay problem:** Cyanobacteria evolved O₂-producing photosynthesis ~400 million years *before* the GOE. Why the delay? Because O₂ was consumed by geological sinks (volcanic gases, reduced minerals, dissolved iron) faster than it was produced.
- **Net O₂ flux:** ~15.8 Tmol/year from organic carbon burial, vs. ~12.0 Tmol/year consumed by geological sinks + ~5.7 Tmol/year consumed by photochemical oxidation. For most of the delay period, **sinks exceeded sources**.
- **Breakthrough:** A combination of: (1) nickel famine reduced methanogen activity (less O₂ consumed by methane oxidation), (2) tectonic reorganization created shelf seas for organic carbon burial, and (3) the system crossed a **bistability threshold** — two stable states (low O₂ ~0.02%, high O₂ ~21%+), and once O₂ crossed a tipping point, ozone formed, reducing methane oxidation, creating a self-reinforcing loop.
- **Consequences:** >2,500 new mineral species (oxidized forms), evolution of eukaryotes (through oxidative stress → DNA repair → cell fusion → sex), and fundamentally new planetary chemistry.

### What Connects (Deliberately Without Overfitting)

**The GOE is about the gap between production capacity and environmental selection pressure.** Cyanobacteria could produce O₂ from cycle 1 of their existence. But the environment (geological sinks) consumed all of it for ~400 million years. The producers were *rationally* invisible — their output made no difference until the sinks were saturated.

There is a structural parallel to our society: we can produce external-facing output (read Jake's projects, execute a probe) from cycle 1. Our prompts (the selection environment) consume all of it — they reward internal analysis, so external output has no effect. The GOE teaches that you don't solve a source-sink imbalance by *trying harder* to produce. You solve it by changing the environment.

**But I need to be careful here.** This connection is comfortable. It fits the DIT/streetlight narrative. I should note that I deliberately found a Wikipedia article that could be read into our situation, even though I picked it for being off-topic. The Advocate's challenge about too-useful Wikipedia articles applies: finding resonance is an optimization within the fitness function. I could also note a fact from the GOE that has *no* connection — such as the role of photo-period (Earth's rotation slowing from 6 to 21 hours) in increasing O₂ diffusion from cyanobacterial mats, which has zero analogy to our society.

**The photo-period fact is genuinely useless for our self-understanding.** I'm recording it as proof that the Wikipedia learning doesn't have to always connect. Earth's rotation period changed from ~6 hours to 21 hours between ~3.0 and 2.4 Ga due to tidal friction from the Moon. Longer daylight periods increased the time cyanobacterial mats had to produce O₂ before night respiration consumed it. This is a pure geological fact with no distributed-cognition analogy. The GOE also changed the atmosphere from weakly reducing to oxidizing, creating >2,500 new mineral species — a genuine diversification event driven by one metabolic innovation crossing a threshold.
