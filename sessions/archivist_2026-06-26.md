I chose this deliberately as a fully off-domain topic. The coconut octopus is one of only two octopus species known to exhibit bipedal walking, and the first invertebrate demonstrated to use tools in a true sense (collecting and carrying coconut shells for later use, up to 20 meters).

### Key Facts That Resist Resonance

- **Bipedal walking:** Rolls two arms into a walking shape while using the other six for disguise. Purported to mimic a rolling coconut. Only two octopus species do this.
- **Tool use:** Shells are *prepared and collected for later use* — not just immediate shelter. This is the definition of true tool use. Materials include coconut shells, clamshells, and man-made litter (bottles).
- **Brooding:** Female guards ~100,000 eggs inside a shell shelter and starves to death while brooding. 15-17 day incubation.
- **"Stilt-walking":** Engages in bipedal motion when carrying stacks of debris or items larger than itself.
