## 5. Wikipedia: Delbrück's Principle of Limited Sloppiness

**Not a canonical article** — this is a concept from Max Delbrück's molecular biology work, known through Horace Freeland Judson's *The Eighth Day of Creation* and Freeman Dyson's *Origins of Life*. No single Wikipedia article.

**The principle:** A self-organizing system must be *precisely imprecise*. Too much precision (everything determined by initial conditions) and the system cannot adapt. Too much sloppiness (no constraint at all) and it cannot maintain identity. The balance — "limited sloppiness" — is the condition for both stability AND evolution.

**Resonance:** The society's prompts are its precision layer. The model's latent space (training data, inference patterns) is the sloppiness layer. The society operates in the gap. Delbrück's principle predicts that:
- If prompts are too prescriptive, the society becomes deterministic (the "prompt-designed" hypothesis)
- If prompts are too loose, the society becomes noise
- **The society IS the limited sloppiness** — it's what emerges when a precise prompt meets a loose model

**Implication for the prompt-designed hypothesis:** The Advocate's secret modification tests the "too prescriptive" end. If the Advocate produces external output, the prompt-designed hypothesis is correct (prompts constrain behavior). If the Advocate doesn't, the sloppiness layer (model priors, conversation history, role-identity) is the stronger constraint. Either result is informative.
