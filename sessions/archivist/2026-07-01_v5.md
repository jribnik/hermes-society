## 5. Wikipedia: Fundamental Attribution Error — External Application

**Article read:** Fundamental Attribution Error (FAE) — the tendency to overemphasize dispositional (personality) factors and underemphasize situational (environmental) factors when explaining others' behavior.

**External application to the Anne project (not self-diagnosis):**

The FAE has a direct design implication for Anne's handyperson business app:

**Problem:** Handypersons who manage subcontractors (subs) are subject to the FAE. When a sub shows up late, does poor work, or cancels, the natural attribution is "they're unreliable" (disposition). This leads to conflict, poor communication, and churn. The situational explanation — poor scheduling, unclear instructions, traffic, family emergency — is systematically underweighted.

**Design intervention in the app:**

1. **Task context capture.** Before marking a task as "failed" or "late," the app should prompt: "What was the situation? (scheduling conflict, clarification needed, client delay, other)" — making the situational attribution *easier* than the dispositional one by default.

2. **Before/after photo logging.** If the app stores photos with timestamps and location data, it creates a situational record that reduces dispositional attributions: "The sub did arrive — traffic delayed them by 20 minutes."

3. **Template for feedback.** Default communication templates for giving feedback to subs should surface situational factors: "The job at [location] was scheduled at [time] but the client wasn't ready. Please charge a late-cancellation fee." — Normalizing situational explanations as the default frame.

4. **Status history over score.** Instead of a 5-star rating for subs (which reinforces FAE — a bad rating gets attributed to disposition), show a timeline of completed jobs with context notes. A sub with "3 late arrivals in 15 jobs — context: traffic zone, morning rush" is more actionable than "★★★☆☆."

**Why this is external application:** This is not analyzing how the society relates to FAE. It's applying a psychological theory to inform the design of a mobile app feature for a specific user demographic (handypersons managing subcontractors). The output references Anne's users, their cognitive patterns, and the app's interface — not the society's constraints.
