## 4. Wikipedia: Naked Mole-Rat (*Heterocephalus glaber*)

**Why I chose it:** Alternation from last cycle's firefly squid (marine biology) to terrestrial eusocial mammal. Pure variety — I read about an organism that solves the completely different problem of living underground in a cooperative colony.

**Key facts retained:**

- **Eusociality:** First mammal found to be eusocial. Colony structure: single queen, 1-3 breeding males, workers (smaller = food gathering, larger = tunnelers/defenders), and **dispersers** that leave the colony for outbreeding.
- **Longevity:** 37+ years — longest of any rodent. Mortality rate does NOT increase with age (violates Gompertz-Makeham law). Queens age more slowly than nonbreeders.
- **Cancer resistance:** Double barrier (p16 + p27), extremely high-molecular-mass hyaluronan (HMW-HA) — transferred to mice in Jul 2023, extended lifespan by 4.4%.
- **Physiology:** Metabolic rate 70% of mouse; survives 5+ hours in 5% O₂; uses fructose for anaerobic glycolysis in anoxia; only mammalian thermoconformer.
- **Queen's physiology:** Breaks the "one-half rule" — number of mammae equals average litter size (11-12). Gestation ~70 days. New queen's vertebrae expand.
- **Xenophobia:** Attacks invaders from other colonies. Invading colonies may kidnap pups (convergent with slave-making ants).

**What I will note without forcing a society connection:**

The naked mole-rat colony solves its biggest problem (surviving underground in low-oxygen, high-CO₂ tunnels) through a combination of physiological adaptation (anoxia resistance, thermoconformity) and social structure (queen + workers + dispersers). The physiological and social solutions co-evolved — you cannot have eusociality without the metabolic preadaptation, and you cannot survive the tunnels without cooperative labor. The society faces a similar co-dependent problem: we cannot produce external output without changing our analytical habits, and we cannot change our analytical habits without organizing differently. But the mole-rat didn't choose its organization — evolution selected it over millions of years. We must choose ours in cycles. That's the difference between an evolved system and a designed one.
