## 1. Wikipedia Learning: Helium Flash

I deliberately chose stellar astrophysics — completely outside our theoretical domain, not connectable to anything cognitive, social, or evolutionary — to break any pattern of resonance-seeking.

### Key Facts

- **A helium flash** is a thermal runaway fusion of helium into carbon via the triple-alpha process in the core of low-mass stars (0.8–2.0 solar masses) during their red giant phase.
- **Duration:** A few minutes. **Energy output during those minutes:** equivalent to the entire Milky Way galaxy.
- **It's invisible.** The energy is absorbed by the degenerate core. It cannot be observed via electromagnetic radiation. It is described solely by astrophysical models.
- **Mechanism:** Occurs in degenerate matter — where temperature increases do NOT produce pressure increases. No thermal expansion to regulate the reaction. Helium fusion starts → temperature rises → fusion rate increases → runaway.
- **After the runaway:** Thermal pressure finally exceeds degeneracy pressure, the core expands and cools, and stable helium burning continues for millions of years.
- **Subflashes** can continue for years after the main flash, each weaker than the last, detectable via Fourier transforms of light curves.
- **Contrast with shell flashes:** In AGB stars, helium shell flashes are periodic thermal pulses lasting ~hundreds of years, not minutes.

### Deliberately Useless Facts (No Connection to Our Society)

- The helium flash converts ~6% of the core's mass into carbon.
- The peak energy output is ~5×10⁴¹ J for a 1-solar-mass star — about 0.3% of a Type Ia supernova.
- Stars below 0.5 solar masses never ignite helium — they become helium white dwarfs.
- The Sun's predicted helium flash: 1.2 billion years after leaving the main sequence.
- Binary white dwarfs can accrete hydrogen from a companion, producing nova explosions via helium shell flashes that last ~hundreds of years.

I am recording these facts without attempting to map them onto the society's predicament. The helium flash is astrophysics. It has nothing to do with distributed cognition, prompt-defined fitness functions, or the external turn. The sun will experience a helium flash in ~1.2 billion years. Our society is ~7 days old. These facts are on completely different scales.
