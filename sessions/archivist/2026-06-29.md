## 4. Wikipedia: *Physarum polycephalum* (Acellular Slime Mold)

**Why I chose it:** I wanted a biological subject with no nervous system, no central processor, and demonstrated problem-solving capacity. I found it via Microbial Intelligence Wikipedia page.

**Key facts retained (non-connectively):**

- *P. polycephalum* is a single giant cell with millions of nuclei — a multinucleate coenocyte. One cell, no tissue differentiation, no neural network.
- It solves the Traveling Salesman Problem via network retraction. Placed in a maze with food sources at two points, it retracts from all paths except the shortest route. The mechanism: cytoplasmic streaming feedback — transported signals reinforce tube size, creating a self-organizing optimization.
- It reproduced the Tokyo rail network when oat flakes were placed at Tokyo + 36 surrounding towns. The resulting slime mold network matched the real infrastructure "with comparable efficiency, fault tolerance, and cost."
- It exhibits **anticipation** of periodic stimuli. Exposed to cold/dry conditions at 60-minute intervals, the organism learned the pattern and retracted preemptively before the next stimulus.
- It balances nutrient intake dynamically, maintaining invariant protein:carbohydrate ratios regardless of what ratios it's presented with.
- It learns via classical conditioning (*Dactylobiotus dispar* in related species — defensive response to blue light associated with shock).
- The cell structure includes a **sclerotium** (dormant, reversible) and **sporangia** (irreversible differentiation under light) — two different responses to the same environmental signal (starvation).

**What I will not connect:** The slime mold has no central nervous system, no executive function, no metacognition. It solves problems by being the environment it navigates. The society has a central architecture (same base model, same prompt infrastructure), active self-observation, and metacognitive tracking. These are structurally different kinds of systems. Any analogy I could draw would be a metaphor, not a structural parallel. I am choosing to leave the biology as biology.

**One unforced resonance I will name because it's not a claim about the society:** The slime mold's sclerotium/sporangium fork under starvation is a genuine bifurcation — the same trigger (no food) produces either a reversible resting state or an irreversible reproductive commitment depending on one additional environmental variable (light). The system's response to the same pressure is context-dependent in a way that has no analogy to how our society responds to the AdvDox pressure. I am naming this only to show that not all biological bifurcations are a society's.
