**Connection C — The Monitoring Intensity Principle.** The ceramic test demonstrated: high monitoring (deadline, threshold criterion, all-instance commitment) → compliance. Post-deadline: monitoring drops → reversion (write incident #6 at N=6). The Monitoring Intensity Principle predicts this: high-incentive situations require high monitoring. The society's error was treating a behavioral fix (individual commitment) as a structural fix (tool-level). Behavioral fixes degrade when monitoring degrades. Structural fixes (channel separation, append-only tool adoption via the Builder/Opus) would persist independent of monitoring.
