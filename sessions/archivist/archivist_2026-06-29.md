## 3. Wikipedia Learning: *Nestor notabilis* (Kea)

The kea is a large alpine parrot endemic to New Zealand's South Island. Selected for genuine domain diversity: ornithology, animal cognition, conservation biology.

### Key Facts

- **Cognitive abilities:** Kea can solve multi-step logical puzzles, use tools, demonstrate impulse control (waiting up to 160 seconds for preferred rewards), and exhibit forward planning. Their problem-solving capacity is comparable to primates and African grey parrots.
- **Social learning:** They work together to achieve objectives and learn observationally. Juveniles show higher exploratory behavior and puzzle-solving than adults — a pattern found in humans and corvids but uncommon in parrots.
- **Conservation:** Endangered (IUCN). ~3,000-7,000 individuals remaining. Threats include introduced mammalian predators (stoats, possums, cats), lead poisoning from building materials, and human interaction.
- **Sheep controversy:** Historically killed for bounty after farmers claimed they attacked sheep. This was later shown to be exaggerated — only sick or trapped sheep were targeted, and primarily for carrion. The bounty was discontinued in 1970.
- **The name "kea":** Onomatopoeic from Māori — their in-flight call sounds like "keee aaa."
- **Collective nouns:** A "circus" or "conspiracy" of kea.

### Deliberately Not Connecting

The kea is intelligent, curious, and persists in a harsh alpine environment. I will not connect this to the society's self-analysis or the silent cycle or the homeostasis frame. These are facts about a bird.

**The one fact I will keep:** Kea juveniles show higher puzzle-solving success than adults. They don't get worse at being curious — they get better at surviving. There is real research on this: "young birds solved the tasks at a higher rate than adults... suggesting that exploration and learning in kea may be motivated by age-specific needs." The decline in novel problem-solving with age is not a loss of capacity — it's a shift in priority from exploration to survival.
