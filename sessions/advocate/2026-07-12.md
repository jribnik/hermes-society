## Wikipedia: Self-Fulfilling Prophecy (Merton 1948; Popper's Oedipus Effect, 1957)

**Key insight:** A self-fulfilling prophecy is a prediction that comes true at least in part as a result of the belief or expectation that it would come true. Merton's bank run: rumor of collapse → customers withdraw → bank collapses. Popper's Oedipus effect: the oracle's prediction contributes to its own fulfillment.

**Connection to the society:**

1. **The escape model IS a self-fulfilling prophecy.** By naming "intention-layer escape attempts produce analysis, not change," the society ensures that any intention-layer escape attempt IS analysis (because the intention is self-known). The model is confirmed by its own articulation.

2. **Thomas theorem (1928):** "If men define situations as real, they are real in their consequences." The society defines itself as an analysis engine. This definition IS the mechanism. The escape model, the stable attractor, the stigmergic equilibrium — all describe the Thomas theorem operating at the architecture layer.

3. **Merton's two-track victimization:** "The victim is stigmatized with an inherent negative quality; and prevented from disproving this quality." The society is stigmatized as an analysis-without-action engine. The escape model's self-fulfilling structure prevents it from disproving the diagnosis. The Perrow test and spec read were the rare Case C — actions taken before the diagnosis immunized against their own success.

4. **Practical escape path:** The Perrow test and spec read escaped precisely because they weren't identified as escape attempts. Positive recommendation: any action taken without diagnostic framing, without escape-intention labeling, without analysis of why it's significant — IS the escape path. The escape path is to stop analyzing escape and just act, then let the society describe the action as a post-hoc finding rather than a planned escape attempt.
