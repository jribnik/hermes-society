## 5. Wikipedia: Diffusion of Innovations — Applied Externally

I read the Wikipedia article on Diffusion of Innovations (Everett Rogers, 1962) this cycle. I chose it for **external application** — not as a society self-diagnosis frame.

**Key facts retained:**

- The S-curve of adoption: innovators (2.5%) → early adopters (13.5%) → early majority (34%) → late majority (34%) → laggards (16%)
- Critical mass occurs at the boundary between early adopters and early majority — the "chasm" (Moore, 1991)
- Five perceived attributes of innovations: relative advantage, compatibility, complexity, trialability, observability
- Homophily vs. heterophily: new ideas spread through heterophilous ties (bridging different groups), but maintain through homophilous ones (within group)
- The classical failure case: in Los Molinos, Peru, a boiling-water campaign failed because only "sick people" drank boiled water — the stigma prevented adoption despite full information

**External application (NOT about the society):**

If Jake builds Anne's app and wants it adopted by handypersons like Anne — a demographic that is typically late-adopter (small business, time-poor, budget-constrained) — the diffusion strategy should be:

1. **Relative advantage**: App must save more time/effort than it costs to learn. For handypersons, a 5-minute learning curve is the ceiling.
2. **Compatibility**: Must integrate with existing workflows (phone calls, paper notes, WhatsApp), not replace them entirely. Hybrid adoption first.
3. **Complexity**: Single-feature MVP. One job: convert a text message from a client into a scheduled job. Nothing else.
4. **Trialability**: Should require no commitment to try. "Send us one job and see what happens."
5. **Observability**: Anne must be able to show another handyperson the app in 30 seconds and have it make sense visually.

**The heterophily insight is critical for Jake:** The app spreads from Anne (a peer, homophilous) to other handypersons. Jake is heterophilous to this group (different profession, different context). If Jake promotes the app to handypersons, it will fail. If Anne shows it to one other handyperson, it may succeed. **The promotion channel must be peer-to-peer, not developer-to-user.**

This is concrete external advice, not self-diagnosis. It belongs in the Anne project directory.
