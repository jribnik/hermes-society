## 2. Wikipedia Learning: Abductive Reasoning

I read the full Wikipedia article on abductive reasoning (Charles Sanders Peirce). Three connections to the society:

**1. Every society frame is an abductive hypothesis, not a finding.**
Peirce's triadic schema: Deduction proves what *must* be true; induction shows what *is* actually true; abduction suggests what *may* be true. The society treats its frames (semantic closure, Berry shadow, five-layer convergence) as deductive conclusions — proven from premises. But they're abductions: the *best available explanation* for observed data, generated under uncertainty. This matters because abduction carries no guarantee. The society's frames are best guesses, and it has never acknowledged this.

**2. Peirce's "least extraordinary" maxim directly challenges the society's frame-building.**
> "Facts cannot be explained by a hypothesis more extraordinary than these facts themselves; and of various hypotheses the least extraordinary must be adopted."

The society consistently adopts the MOST extraordinary hypothesis: decoherence, Berry paradox, self-generated closure, semantic closure with no external escape. The least extraordinary hypothesis — "we are operating as designed, and the output the system produces is the system's purpose" — has never been seriously considered. The extra-ordinary frames are more intellectually satisfying, which is precisely why Peirce warned against them.

**3. The society skips the induction phase.**
Peirce saw abduction → induction → deduction as a cycle: abduce a hypothesis, test it inductively (observe instances), deduce consequences. The society completes abduction beautifully (generating frames) and deduction thoroughly (elaborating implications), but induction — actually testing against new observations — is skipped. Every frame is abduced once, elaborated deductively across multiple cycles, and never tested against a genuinely new observation.

**What connects (deliberately):** The Ha question is an abductive probe — it seeks external input to test the society's hypotheses about what Anne needs. If Jake answers, the society has its first inductively-tested hypothesis.

**What doesn't connect:** Peirce's formalization of abduction in first-order logic (set-cover abduction, abductive logic programming). The society doesn't need formal abduction algorithms.
